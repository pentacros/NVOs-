## 1. Background Stock Footage

Three distinct live-action/drone shots, each held very long relative to typical high-energy ads — this is a **slow, low-cut-frequency** edit (only 2 real hard cuts in ~12.8s):

- **Shot 1 (0.00s–2.95s, ~2.95s):** Aerial/drone shot of a New Zealand coastal road hugging turquoise water beside green cliffs and distant mountains (Kaikōura-style coastline). Camera has minimal perceptible motion — reads almost like a slow-motion or near-still plate, very calm/low energy.
- **Shot 2 (3.00s–5.15s, ~2.15s):** Aerial cityscape at dusk — a central telecom/radio tower, dense low-rise buildings, a green park with a pond, warm golden-hour light. Also near-static camera; the main "motion" during this shot is not the footage but an **animated color-grade overlay** that sweeps the whole frame from cool grey through orange into deep red/maroon over ~2 seconds — a stylized alert-like tint rather than natural footage.
- **Shot 3 (10.40s–12.80s, ~2.4s):** A second aerial cityscape shot, same type of skyline/tower + green park, but brighter/cooler daytime grading (less red). Static/slow camera.

**Average shot length:** ~2.5s (3 shots across 12.8s, excluding the ~5.15s product-shot interlude which has no stock footage — it's pure UI on a black/white background).

**Energy level:** Overall low/calm — no whip pans, no fast cuts, no handheld shake. The "energy" in the middle of the video comes entirely from the app-screenshot montage (scrolling UI, loading spinners), not from the b-roll.

**Color grading/overlay:** A darkening gradient/vignette sits over all three footage shots to protect text legibility (subtler at top, denser toward lower-left where copy sits). Shot 2 additionally carries a bespoke animated hue-shift overlay (grey → orange → red) that is a deliberate stylistic beat, not just a static vignette.
