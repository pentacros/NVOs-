# Aspect Ratio Comparison — "Singapore NVO A - under 60 secs"

Baseline: 9:16 (1080x1920, 277 frames, 13.85s). Compared against 16:9 (1920x1080) and 1:1 (1080x1080), same duration/frame count.

## 1. Background footage — PILLARBOX WITH BLURRED FILL (confirmed)
Same 4 shots (MBS towers → street traffic dusk → lawn/campus tower → Merlion Park), same order. In 16:9, cut points land 0.5-0.9s *later* than the 9:16 baseline (growing offset through the video — a real retime, not rounding). In 1:1, cut points land at the exact same frame as the 9:16 baseline. Both non-vertical ratios use blur-pillarbox (native footage sharp in a center column, blurred zoomed duplicate on the sides).

## 2. Text/pill stack (headline + 5 benefit pills)
Content and pill colors identical across ratios, but **timing is NOT simply reframed — it's independently retimed per ratio**:
- 16:9: headline + pills 1-4 land within 0.05s of baseline; pill 5 ("5-hour flight from home") lands ~1s later than baseline (7.05s vs 6.00s). Full stack clears in lockstep with the shot3→shot4 cut (~11.45s), leaving ~0.8s of clean b-roll before the CTA — a genuine structural difference vs. baseline (which holds text continuously across that cut).
- 1:1: pills 1, 4, and 5 all land later than baseline (by ~0.75-1.6s each); pills 2-3 coincidentally match. Full stack clears ~1s *earlier* than baseline (~11.15-11.2s vs ~12.15s), giving a longer CTA hold (~2.3s vs less in baseline).
- Left margin also differs: ~2-3% in 9:16/16:9 vs. ~8-10% in 1:1 (text appears more inset, aligned to where the sharp reframed footage begins rather than the canvas edge).

## 3. CTA
Same copy/styling ("GET YOUR / FREE SHORTLIST NOW", blue pill, white+gold text, fast slide+blur entrance) in all 3 ratios; only entrance timecode and hold-duration shift per the retiming noted above. Position reads mid-lower/left-of-center in all ratios, not bottom-anchored.

## 4. Safe-area rule
Weaker/less consistent in this campaign than others checked — the pillarbox framing rule holds for footage, but the *text layer timing* was evidently hand-retouched per ratio rather than mechanically reframed, unlike Germany/Dubai where timing was frame-identical across ratios. Treat this campaign as the outlier confirming that cross-ratio consistency is an editor habit, not a hard platform rule.

## 5. Missing/added/repositioned elements
No elements added/removed — same 4 background shots, same headline+5 pills, same CTA in all ratios. No logos in this campaign (Singapore A has no university/employer logos at all, in any ratio).
