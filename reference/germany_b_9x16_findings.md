## 1. Background Stock Footage

Three distinct shots, all handheld travel/lifestyle b-roll, no scripted actors, very low cut frequency (2 cuts in 11.26s) and minimal camera movement — this is a **slow, calm-paced** cutdown, not energetic. No visible directional camera motion (pans/tilts); only natural handheld micro-shake and ambient subject motion (pedestrians, wind in leaves, a tram driving through frame). No darkening gradient, vignette, or scrim color-grading overlay was applied over the footage for legibility — text instead sits on solid-color pill/box backgrounds rather than a footage treatment. Footage colors are bright, naturally saturated, daylight exposure, slightly soft/compressed (typical phone/stock-footage sharpness, not cinematic grain).

- **Shot 1 — Reichstag, Berlin** (0.00–2.35s, ~2.4s): Static wide shot of the Reichstag building with German flag flying on a pole, small knot of tourists in the plaza below, autumn tree branches with yellowing leaves hanging into frame from the top (natural foreground framing device). Camera essentially locked off with tiny handheld sway.
- **Shot 2 — Heidelberg Old Bridge Gate** (2.40–7.35s, ~4.95s): Static/near-static shot looking through the twin-towered bridge gate (Karlstor), a woman in a purple dress walking away from camera, a stone statue on a plinth, a parked teal e-scooter in the foreground, wooded hills in the distance. This is the longest hold of the ad.
- **Shot 3 — European old-town tram square** (7.40–11.26s, ~3.8s): Cobblestone/checkerboard-paved square, a vintage cream-and-red tram driving through the frame (the one shot with clear subject motion), period buildings and a church spire in the background.

Average shot length ≈ 3.75s. Overall energy: low/calm — long static holds, gentle ambient motion, no whip pans, no fast cutting.

## 3. Highlight Styling

- **Slide 1 headline** ("Germany Masters"): highlighted via a bold red type color plus an irregular white "brush-stroke"/paint-swipe shape behind it (not a clean rectangle — a torn-edge/marker-stroke box), giving a marker-highlight feel.
- **Slide 1 sub-line & Slide 2 & Slide 3**: highlighted via solid-fill rounded rectangle "pill" backgrounds (flat indigo/blue, ~#2A0FE0, no gradient, no border, soft-rounded corners ~8–12px equivalent), white text on top — this pill treatment is the dominant highlight device across the whole ad.
- **Slide 2 internal contrast**: "Universities" is bolted-down/bold non-italic against the surrounding italic serif copy — a weight+style contrast highlight rather than a color change.
- **CTA slide**: the phrase "Germany University Shortlist" is recolored to yellow against the white "Get your Personalised" — a pure color-swap highlight (no pill-within-pill, no underline, no circle/marker shapes anywhere in the ad).
- No underlines, no circled words, no animated wipe/reveal masks were observed anywhere — all highlighting is either (a) solid pill backgrounds, (b) color swap, or (c) weight/style contrast.

## 7. Transitions

- **Footage cuts**: hard cuts only (Reichstag → Heidelberg at 2.40s; Heidelberg → tram square at 7.35s). No crossfades, whip-pans, or zooms between shots.
- **Text-slide transitions**: soft opacity fades/dissolves, built progressively phrase-by-phrase (each new word/phrase fades in over roughly 0.15–0.35s while prior copy stays static), then the whole block holds; outgoing slides simply fade out (or cut out abruptly, as with Slide 2→Slide 3 gap at 8.15–8.40s where there's a brief text-free moment). No slide-in/slide-out or pop/bounce motion was observed on any text or logo element.

## 8. Other Stylistic Devices

- **Natural foreground framing**: autumn tree branches/leaves hang into the top of frame during Shots 1 and 2, acting as a soft natural vignette/frame without being a deliberate post-production vignette.
- **Safe margins**: text/pill blocks consistently sit with ~5% left margin and ~3–5% top margin; nothing bleeds to the frame edge.
- **No letterboxing**: full 9:16 bleed throughout.
- **No visible film grain, light leaks, or added noise** — footage reads as clean handheld/stock video.
- **Consistent brand blue** (#2A0FE0-ish indigo) is the single unifying highlight color across all pill/box treatments except the Slide-1 headline (red-on-white) and the CTA's yellow accent — i.e., the ad uses one accent blue as its primary "system color" plus two one-off exceptions for emphasis.
