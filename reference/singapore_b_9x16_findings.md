## 2. Background Stock Footage

Five distinct shots, average shot length ≈ **3.7s** (slower-paced than typical short NVO cuts — few, longer holds rather than rapid cutting):

1. **0.00–5.60s (5.6s)** — Night aerial/drone shot of Singapore's Marina Bay CBD skyline: lit skyscrapers, highway/river below. Camera is essentially static with only the faintest drift — very low energy, moody. Heavy dark navy/near-black color grade with what reads as a top-down gradient/vignette darkening for text legibility.
2. **5.60–11.55s (5.95s)** — Daytime street-level shot looking steeply upward at a glass office tower with a palm tree in frame; slow handheld/drone tilt-pan gives subtle continuous motion (parallax) rather than a cut. Medium-low energy. Natural daylight blue-sky grading, mild vignette, no heavy darkening (text legibility comes from drop shadows/glow instead).
3. **11.55–13.20s (1.65s)** — Wide, mostly static shot of the Merlion statue with Marina Bay Sands in the background and its iconic water jet spouting continuously. Motion energy comes only from the moving water. Bright, slightly overcast daylight grade.
4. **13.20–14.80s (1.6s)** — Street-level park/boardwalk shot: pedestrians walking, a child on a kick-scooter, skyline in the background, later transitioning into a pink bougainvillea flower hedge lining the path. Camera slow-pans; medium energy from pedestrian motion.
5. **14.80–18.39s (3.59s, to end)** — Interior of a Gardens-by-the-Bay-style conservatory dome: triangulated blue glass ceiling, a swirling ring/loop walkway structure with a cascading indoor waterfall, dense greenery. Cuts are all **hard cuts** — no crossfades between background shots. Overall the piece is comparatively low-energy/cinematic rather than fast-cut hype editing; only 4 cut points across 18.39s.

## 4. Highlight Styling

Several distinct highlight techniques are used, layered by slide:

- **Title-word highlight ("Singapore", "Masters")**: not a background pill — the highlight is achieved entirely via **typeface + color + glow substitution**. Plain white italic serif ("Get Your") switches to a bold cursive/script font rendered in an orange-to-red gradient fill with a dark red/black outer glow (like an embossed neon sign). This is the single strongest visual signature of the ad.
- **"Admit in 21 Days"**: highlighted with a **solid red underline** running the full width of the phrase, plus a ⚡ lightning-bolt emoji glyph appended — a literal "urgency" marker rather than a color change.
- **"Across Universities Like"**: highlighted via straight **color swap** to lime/yellow-green against the surrounding white "Get"/"Shortlist" text — no pill, no underline, just a saturated color contrast.
- **Checklist item rows ("Admit Eligibility", "Top Jobs After Graduation", "Expenses & Earnings")**: each sits inside a **rounded, full-pill lavender/light-purple background box**, with a **solid purple circular numbered badge** (white "1"/"2"/"3" on filled purple) fixed to the left edge of each pill. This is the one true "pill/badge" highlight device in the video.
- **Toggle switches**: each checklist row ends in an **iOS-style toggle switch** — starts as an empty grey/white circle-in-track, then animates to a solid purple-filled track with the knob slid to the right ("ON" state), sequentially for items 1→2→3. This doubles as a highlight/emphasis animation communicating "verified/eligible."
- **CTA text**: two-tone highlight — "Check Your" in white, "Admit Eligibility Now" in yellow, both on a solid blue pill/box background (see Section 5).

## 5. CTA

- **Text**: "Check Your" (line 1, white) / "Admit Eligibility Now" (line 2, yellow), bold sans-serif, both lines inside one solid blue rounded-rectangle/pill-shaped box with soft corners.
- **Timecode**: appears ~17.40s, fully formed ~17.55s, holds to end (18.39s).
- **Position**: lower-left third of frame, left-aligned within safe margins.
- **Styling vs. body copy**: unlike every other text block (which floats directly over footage with only a drop shadow), the CTA is the only text wrapped in a **solid-fill background container** (opaque blue), giving it clear button affordance. No visible pulsing/looping animation — it is a static hold once formed.

## 7. Product Shot

- There is no traditional product shot, app screenshot, or device mockup (no phone frame, no floating card with drop shadow depicting an app UI).
- However, the **checklist UI (Section 3/4)** functions as a lightweight, native-feeling "app-like" overlay: numbered purple-badge rows with pill backgrounds and animated iOS-style toggle switches, floating directly on top of the live-action footage (not inside a device frame) — essentially a UI-mockup graphic composited straight onto the background video, positioned top/upper-left, roughly 25–30% of frame height. It does not replace or box out the background footage; it sits on top of it with a drop shadow for legibility, non-obtrusive picture-in-picture style.

## 8. Transitions

- **Between background footage shots**: all **hard cuts** (5 shots, 4 cut points at 5.60s, 11.55s, 13.20s, 14.80s). No crossfades, whip-pans, or zoom transitions between shots.
- **Between text slides**: text blocks build progressively via **fade-in / wipe-in** per line (not simultaneous), and old slides are cleared abruptly at the same instant as the background hard-cut (text slide changes are synced 1:1 with footage cuts, except for the "Check:" checklist which persists unchanged across 3 footage cuts).
- **Within a slide**, individual lines/words use: fade-in (most lines), typewriter reveal ("Admit in 21 Days"), and wipe-from-outline-to-fill (the orange gradient "Singapore"/"Masters" treatment).
- **Checklist → CTA**: a brief **cross-dissolve** (~0.1–0.15s) rather than a hard cut — the only true dissolve transition in the video.
- **Toggle switches** animate with a smooth slide/fill motion (knob translates + track fill color transitions), each ~0.1–0.2s, staggered ~0.2s apart.

## 9. Other Stylistic Devices

- **Consistent drop shadow** on all text (both the italic serif lead-ins and the colored highlight words) for legibility against varied footage — this substitutes for a dark scrim/overlay in the daytime shots.
- **Heavier dark gradient/vignette overlay** only on the opening night skyline shot; daytime shots rely on text shadow/glow alone.
- **Two consistent font families** run through the whole video: (1) an italic serif/script for all lead-in words — "Get Your", "Get", "Check:" — and (2) a bold sans-serif for supporting/functional copy (dates, checklist items, CTA). The brand-name highlight ("Singapore"/"Masters") uses a third, distinct bold cursive/slab display font exclusively for those two words.
- **Left-aligned safe margins** (~5% from left edge) maintained throughout for all text and logo placements; bottom ~15–20% of frame is generally kept clear of text (kept for platform UI/captions in social placements).
- **No letterboxing** — full 9:16 bleed throughout.
- **No visible film grain, light leaks, or noise overlays** — the grade is clean/digital, aside from the natural video compression/motion blur of the source footage.
- **UI-mockup color language**: purple/lavender used consistently for the "checklist" interactive element and blue used exclusively for the final CTA button — a distinct color-coding between "in-progress/informational" (purple) and "action" (blue) states.
