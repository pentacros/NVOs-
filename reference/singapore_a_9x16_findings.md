## 3. Highlight Styling

There isn't a "highlighted word within a sentence" pattern (e.g., no single bold/underlined word inside a longer sentence) — instead, **each entire benefit line is its own highlight**, styled as a solid-color pill:

- **Headline** ("1-year Masters"): no pill — plain white bold text with a soft dark drop-shadow for legibility against the sky/foliage. This is the only line that is not pilled.
- **First benefit** ("Asia's finance capital"): solid **orange/tomato pill** (~#FF5A1F–#FF6A00 range), fully rounded rectangle corners (~8–10px radius relative to pill height), white bold text, tight horizontal padding.
- **Remaining 4 benefits + CTA container**: solid **royal/cobalt blue pill** (~#1E3FFF–#2B4EFF range), same rounded-rectangle shape and padding as the orange one, white bold text — visually a repeating "chip" component, just recolored blue for everything after the first highlight.
- **CTA**: same blue pill shape/color as the benefit chips, but the text inside uses two different colors on two lines (white top line, yellow/gold bottom line) and italic all-caps type, distinguishing it from the sentence-case benefit chips.

No underline, no strikethrough, no circle/marker highlights, no animated wipe-in on the text fill itself — the "animation" is the pill's pop-in, not a text treatment.

To recreate in CSS: each benefit is a `<span>`/`<div>` with `background-color`, `border-radius`, `padding: 4px 10px`-ish, `font-weight:700`, `color:#fff`, appearing via a quick `scale`/`opacity` pop transition (no easing overshoot visible, looks like a simple fast fade+scale, not a bounce).

## 8. Other Stylistic Notes

- No film grain, no light leaks, no letterboxing — full-bleed 9:16 throughout.
- No dark gradient/vignette scrim over footage; legibility relies solely on the pill backgrounds + drop-shadow on the headline.
- Very low edit/cut frequency (3 cuts in ~14s, average shot length ~3.45s) contrasted with a fairly "busy," constantly-growing text stack — the pacing energy in this ad comes almost entirely from copy accumulation, not visual cutting.
- Consistent two-color brand system across all pills: orange for the single "hook" stat, blue for every subsequent supporting benefit and the CTA — an easy palette to reproduce (`#FF6A00`-ish orange, `#1E3FFF`-ish blue, white and yellow/gold text).
- The benefit stack persists across all 3 background cuts unmodified — i.e., the text layer is fully decoupled from the footage layer, which simplifies rebuilding this as an HTML/CSS composition (a fixed-position text-stack component overlaying a swappable video/background layer).
